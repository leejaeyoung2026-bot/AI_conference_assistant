Technological Insight

Evolution of Speech Recognition (ASR): From HMM to End-to-End Neural Networks

Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) has made significant progress over the past decades. Early speech recognition systems were based on Hidden Markov Models (HMM) and Gaussian Mixture Models (GMM), distinguishing sound units in a statistical way. However, this approach had limitations in handling complex background noise or various accents.

Introduction of Neural Network Technology

Deep Learning was introduced in the 2010s. In particular, Recurrent Neural Networks (RNN) and LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) structures excelled at processing time-series data like speech, and recently, models based on the Transformer structure have shown overwhelming accuracy.

The Web Speech API adopted by this service implements these modern speech recognition technologies as a browser standard. It has the advantage of providing immediate feedback on the user's device while maintaining a high recognition rate through real-time communication with cloud servers.

Artificial Intelligence

Principles of Contextual Correction for Transcription Results Using LLMs

It is difficult for a speech recognition engine to output 100% perfect text. Especially when pronouncing technical terms like 'API', the system struggles between recognizing it as 'API' or writing it exactly as it sounds. This is where the role of the Large Language Model (LLM), Gemini AI, becomes important.

Gemini AI does more than just fix words; it understands the context of the entire sentence. If the meeting topic is 'IT Projects', 'API' should naturally be corrected, and the confidence of the correction increases if words like 'database' or 'endpoint' appear in preceding or succeeding sentences.

Value of Intelligent Summarization

'Insight' is more important than simple text recording. Gemini AI analyzes real-time transcription data to automatically extract questions and proposes action items by summarizing the entire content towards the end of the meeting. This provides value as a strategic decision support tool beyond a simple recording tool.

Core Technology

VORA's Intelligent Engine: Principles of the Real-time Summary Feedback Loop

The biggest limitation of traditional speech recognition systems is the 'disconnection of context'. As conversations get longer, AI often forgets important topics discussed earlier or fails to identify what the currently mentioned 'it' refers to. To solve this, VORA introduced the 'Real-time Summary Feedback Loop' technology.

1. Dynamic Context Updates Every Minute

VORA generates a core summary by compressing the conversation every minute as the meeting progresses. This summary is not just for the user's benefit. The generated summary data is **immediately re-injected back into the AI's system prompt**. This is like updating the AI with the latest memory of 'what we are currently discussing' every minute.

2. High-Precision Contextual Correction Based on Summary

Typos in the speech recognition process often come from a lack of association with surrounding words. VORA's correction engine doesn't just look at individual words. Based on the overall context secured through the re-injected summary, it probabilistically selects and corrects terms that are **most appropriate for the current business situation** among numerous candidates with similar pronunciations.

3. Token Efficiency and Long-term Memory Retention

Sending all text scripts of a long meeting to the AI every time is technically impossible or very inefficient. By utilizing refined 'core summaries' as feedback instead of the entire script, VORA is designed to maintain consistent logic and provide insights without the AI getting exhausted even in long conversations spanning several hours.

This feedback loop technology is the core driver that makes VORA more than just a dictation app, transforming it into a 'Digital Oracle' that perfectly understands the flow of meetings.

Security & Privacy

Data Security in Corporate Environments: Differences in API Plan Policies

The most important factor many companies consider when adopting AI is 'data sovereignty'. This is because meeting contents are a company's core asset and confidential. This service operates through a Google Gemini API key directly issued by the user, and the data security level is determined by the user's API plan.

According to Google AI Studio's policy, when using an API with paid billing (Paid Tier), all input meeting texts are not used for model training, ensuring complete independence. On the other hand, with the Free Plan (Free Tier), there is a possibility they might be used for service improvement training, so we recommend using a paid plan for official meetings where security is important.

Privacy-by-Design

We do not collect or store users' voice or transcribed text data on our own servers under any circumstances. All data processing takes place through direct communication (HTTPS) between the user's browser and Google's secure servers.
